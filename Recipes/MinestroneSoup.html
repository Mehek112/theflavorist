Minestrone Soup

"A comforting and hearty Italian soup filled with vegetables, beans, and pasta."

Recipe submitted by Mehek

Ingredients

2 tbsp olive oil
1 onion, chopped
2 cloves garlic, minced
2 carrots, diced
2 celery stalks, diced
1 zucchini, diced
1 potato, peeled and diced
1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
1 can (15 oz) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
4 cups vegetable broth
1 cup elbow macaroni (or any small pasta)
1 tsp dried oregano
1 tsp dried basil
Salt and pepper to taste
1 cup spinach leaves
1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Calories

45 kcal (per serving of soup without cheese)
80 kcal (1/4 cup grated Parmesan)
100 kcal (1 cup vegetable broth)
150 kcal (1 cup cooked pasta)
25 kcal (carrots)
20 kcal (1 can kidney beans)
20 kcal (1 can cannellini beans)

Directions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add chopped onion and garlic, and saut√© until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add carrots, celery, zucchini, and potato. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Stir in diced tomatoes, beans, vegetable broth, and bring to a simmer.
  4. Add dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15-20 minutes, until vegetables are tender.
  5. In a separate pot, cook the pasta according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  6. Add the cooked pasta to the soup, and stir in the spinach leaves. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
  7. Serve hot, and garnish with grated Parmesan cheese if desired.
