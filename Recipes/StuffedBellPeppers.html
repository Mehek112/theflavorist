Stuffed Bell Peppers

"Colorful bell peppers filled with a savory mixture of rice, beans, vegetables, and spices – a hearty vegetarian meal!"

Recipe submitted by Mehek

Ingredients

4 large bell peppers (red, yellow, or green)
1 cup cooked rice (white or brown)
1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
1/2 cup corn kernels (frozen or fresh)
1/2 cup diced tomatoes
1/2 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or mozzarella, optional for a vegan version)
1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
1 tsp cumin powder
1 tsp chili powder
1/2 tsp garlic powder
Salt and pepper to taste
1 tbsp olive oil
1/4 cup vegetable broth or water

Calories

120 kcal (per stuffed pepper)
50 kcal (rice)
90 kcal (black beans)
40 kcal (corn)
20 kcal (tomatoes)
80 kcal (cheese)
5 kcal (cilantro)
5 kcal (spices)
10 kcal (olive oil)
5 kcal (vegetable broth)

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Slice the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Set aside.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add diced tomatoes and cook for 2-3 minutes, until soft.
  4. Add the cooked rice, black beans, corn, cilantro, cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine, and cook for 2-3 minutes until heated through.
  5. Stuff each bell pepper with the rice and bean mixture, pressing down gently to pack the filling in.
  6. Place the stuffed peppers in a baking dish. Pour vegetable broth or water around the peppers to prevent burning.
  7. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.
  8. If using cheese, remove the foil in the last 5 minutes of baking and sprinkle cheese over the stuffed peppers. Return to the oven to melt the cheese.
  9. Once the peppers are tender and the cheese is melted (if using), remove from the oven. Serve hot, garnished with extra cilantro if desired.
