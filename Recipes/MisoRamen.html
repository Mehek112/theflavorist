Miso Ramen

"A delicious and nourishing Japanese noodle soup made with miso broth, vegetables, and toppings, perfect for vegetarians!"

Recipe submitted by Mehek

Ingredients

2 cups vegetable broth
2 tbsp white or red miso paste
2 tbsp soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
1 tsp sesame oil
2 servings ramen noodles (use gluten-free noodles if needed)
1/2 cup tofu, cubed (optional)
1/2 cup mushrooms (shiitake or button), sliced
1/2 cup spinach or bok choy
1/4 cup corn kernels (optional)
2 boiled eggs (optional for vegetarians who eat eggs)
1/2 cup sliced green onions
1 sheet nori (seaweed) for garnish
1 tbsp chili oil (optional)
1 tsp garlic, minced
Salt to taste

Calories

180 kcal (broth with miso)
250 kcal (ramen noodles)
90 kcal (tofu)
50 kcal (mushrooms)
30 kcal (spinach)
50 kcal (corn)
70 kcal (boiled egg, optional)
10 kcal (green onions)
5 kcal (nori)

Directions

  1. In a large pot, heat sesame oil over medium heat and saut√© garlic for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Add the vegetable broth and bring it to a simmer.
  3. Stir in the miso paste and soy sauce, ensuring the paste dissolves completely in the broth.
  4. Simmer for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Meanwhile, cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  6. Once the broth is ready, add the mushrooms, tofu (if using), spinach, and corn (if using). Simmer for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  7. To serve, divide the cooked ramen noodles into bowls. Pour the hot miso broth and vegetables over the noodles.
  8. Top with green onions, nori, and a drizzle of chili oil (optional).
  9. Serve hot and enjoy your vegetarian miso ramen!
